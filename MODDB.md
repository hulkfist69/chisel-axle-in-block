## Full description

Hiding an axle inside a block is great for tidy builds. Chiseling is great for
detail. Until now you couldn't have both — the instant you chiseled a block with
an axle in it, the axle (and your power) vanished.

**Chisel Encased Axles** removes that limitation. Encase an axle, then carve the
block around it however you like. The shaft keeps turning, the network stays
connected, and your power room finally looks as good as it works.

Because the carved block is a *real* chiseled block under the hood, you get the
**entire vanilla chisel toolset** — every mode, every size, rotation, and
**multiple materials in the same block** — with a working axle running straight
through it.

### What you can do

- **Carve a live axle.** Encase an axle in any block, then chisel it. It keeps
  spinning and stays powered — no network break, no lost rotation.
- **Use the full chisel.** Modes, sizes, rotation and multi-material all work,
  exactly like a normal chiseled block.
- **Slap a finished design onto an axle.** Hold a pre-chiseled block, wrench in
  the offhand, right-click the axle — it instantly takes that shape with the axle
  preserved inside.
- **Get your stuff back.** Break it and both the chiseled block and the axle drop.
- **See the shaft.** The wooden axle shows through wherever you carve it open.

### How to use it

1. Place an axle and connect it to your power network.
2. **Wrench in the offhand, a block in your main hand** — sprint and right-click
   the axle to encase it (base-game feature).
3. Grab your **chisel** (**hammer in the offhand**, as always) and carve away.
   The axle never stops spinning.

Shortcut: hold a **pre-chiseled block** instead of a plain one in step 2 to stamp
that exact carved shape straight onto the axle.

### Requirements

- Vintage Story **1.22**
- No configuration needed — works with the base-game wrench, mechanical power
  and chiseling systems out of the box
- Universal: install on both client and server
